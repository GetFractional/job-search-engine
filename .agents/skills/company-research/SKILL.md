# Company Research Skill

## Project Defaults
- Start with `job-search-scenarios` when the user asks to find jobs, score jobs, apply to a job, work in Teal, use the Teal Chrome extension, or operate Chrome for job-search work.
- Use Google Chrome for Teal, LinkedIn, job boards, company career sites, and application forms when login state, Cloudflare, challenge prompts, or extension behavior matter.
- Keep Teal as the operating system when the scenario requires pipeline, notes, Excitement, assets, contacts, or follow-ups.
- Preserve claim safety with the Canonical Profile and Metrics Ledger before external-facing metrics, bullets, cover letters, application answers, or outreach.
- Stop before application submission, outreach, references, sensitive voluntary self-ID, or external compensation negotiation unless the user explicitly approves.

## Purpose
Research the company enough to diagnose why the role exists and how Matt should position himself.

## Required Sources
1. `templates/company-research-brief.md`
2. `docs/job-search-operating-system.md`
3. `/Users/mattdimock/Downloads/01_matt_dimock_canonical_profile.md`
4. `/Users/mattdimock/Downloads/04_story_bank.md`

## Inputs
- Company
- Role
- JD
- Company website
- Recent news or funding
- Teal record

## Process
1. Summarize product, customer, business model, pricing, stage, and size.
2. Identify growth signals, market pressure, and hiring triggers.
3. Diagnose why this role likely exists.
4. Infer likely KPIs, risks, and 6 to 12 month success outcomes.
5. Map Matt's strongest proof themes to the company problem.
6. Research the target role, application questions, hiring manager/recruiter, market, competitors, and category pressure when the user is applying to a role.
7. Separate researched facts from inference.

## Output
- Company snapshot
- Market context
- Role diagnosis
- Fit analysis
- Positioning strategy
- Asset plan

## Safety
Use web research when current facts matter. Clearly label inferences.
