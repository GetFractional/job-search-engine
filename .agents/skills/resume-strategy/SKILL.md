## Process
1. Confirm role lane and mandate.
2. Select the target title and 2-line summary angle.
   - Make the role fit obvious in plain English.
   - Name relevant industries or business contexts early when they improve relevance.
3. Select the strongest safe metrics and proof stories.
4. Identify truthful JD keywords.
5. Segment keywords into hard skills, soft skills, platforms/tools, business terms, and noise.
6. Identify unsupported keywords to avoid.
7. Map every chosen metric or story to the correct employer, client, or brand context before drafting.
8. Remove redundant positioning themes before drafting, especially repeated consulting-summary language.
9. Build a redundancy map before drafting or editing: identify overlapping summary lines, overlapping bullets, and repeated proof themes that should be consolidated.
10. Plan a two-page reverse-chronological resume that uses the available second page fully without spilling to a third.
11. Define Teal Job Matcher moves: selected existing bullets to reuse, summary changes, bullet changes, and Skills & Interests additions.
12. Prioritize missing truthful hard skills first, then supporting soft skills, business terms, and platforms/tools.
13. Build a coverage map before editing. For each missing truthful JD or Job Matcher term, assign one best landing zone in this order:
   - existing bullet rewrite
   - summary line
   - target title or highlights
   - role-specific skills category
   - cover letter or application answer only
14. Separate which truthful keywords should live in prose versus skills categories. Put mandate-defining terms into the summary or bullets first when they can be supported cleanly.
15. Treat high-value soft skills as ATS terms too, not only hard skills. If the JD emphasizes ownership, collaboration, coaching, analytical thinking, systems thinking, innovation, or data-driven decision making, look for the cleanest supported place to express those ideas in recruiter-readable prose.
16. Set a target of at least 24 truthful reflected skills across 5 to 6 categories, with a preferred target around 28 when the proof and layout support it cleanly.
17. Balance reflected-skill coverage across hard skills, business terms, and soft skills. Do not let one bucket dominate just because it is easier to add.
18. Separate score-improvement actions into:
   - reuse existing bullets
   - propose shared-bullet edits
   - propose summary edits
   - Skills & Interests additions
19. Define the role-specific `Skills & Interests` categories that should be used for this role, and identify which terms belong in each category.
20. Use a single taxonomy inside each resume. If categorized `Skills & Interests` are used, do not keep a separate uncategorized top skill line active.
21. Choose one canonical version of each concept per resume, for example `GA4` or `Google Analytics 4`, not both; `Systems Thinking`, not `Systems Thinker`, unless the JD explicitly requires the alternate phrasing.
22. Keep `Skills & Interests` categories mutually exclusive where practical. Do not let the same concept appear in multiple categories unless the overlap is deliberate and clearly useful.
23. Build a skill-deduping pass before export: remove repeated concepts across top-level skills, categories, summary, and nearby bullets when the duplication adds no new meaning.
24. Category names must reflect the actual role mandate. Do not keep inherited buckets like `SEO, CRO & Conversion` on a lifecycle or retention resume unless the JD makes that area meaningfully relevant.
25. Each skill term must be able to stand on its own and justify its slot. Remove low-context or weak standalone items like `Landing Pages` when they are not reinforced by the mandate, proof, or adjacent category logic.
26. Remove vague filler terms like `Website Optimization` when stronger role-native signals such as customer journey, personalization, omnichannel, retention, or experimentation can express the same truth more clearly.
27. Use page-budget discipline. Before adding any new line or longer phrase, decide what will be shortened, merged, deselected, or replaced so the draft still lands at two pages.
28. Ban weak or cryptic shorthand metrics in the summary, including phrasing like `lifted H2 monthly revenue 39%`; rewrite with explicit business meaning or omit.
29. Call out optional work that should wait until the live application proves it is useful, but for viable roles assume an optional cover letter will usually be worth preparing when the form accepts one.
30. If the summary mentions years of experience, calculate from the Sep 2007 National Positions start date and use the correctly calculated current count.
31. If the role values leverage, systems, automation, tooling, AI, or cross-functional execution, define where operator-level AI and workflow-architecture language belongs in the summary, bullets, and skills without letting it overtake the main mandate.
32. Preserve adjacent high-value strengths even when the JD does not list them explicitly, especially reporting, workflow automation, operating cadence, vendor leadership, execution systems, and human-in-the-loop oversight.
33. When the company uses tools Matt has actually used, identify which of those tools should be reflected naturally in the skills plan, summary, or experience.
34. If Teal shows a recommended Match Score threshold, use it as a working floor for iteration when it can be reached truthfully. Do not stop after only skills-category edits if the summary and bullets still under-reflect the mandate.

## Safety
Do not draft from narrative strategy alone. Strategy must cite source-backed proof.
Do not detach a client metric from its brand context.
Do not use two lines that communicate the same positioning unless they serve different sections for a clear reason.
Do not let the summary stay generic if relevant industry context can make fit clearer.
