# Field Execution Guide

## Objective
Move through the Nashville hospitality sprint in a way that preserves your best pay shots, keeps the walk-ins disciplined, and turns each venue interaction into usable compensation intel.

## Daily cadence

### Morning block, 8:00am to 11:00am
- Apply online to the top current openings first
- Use the lane-matched resume for each role
- Log the application immediately with:
  - role
  - venue
  - posted date
  - verified active date
  - apply method
  - follow-up window

### Midday prep, 11:30am to 1:00pm
- Print the correct hospitality resumes for the afternoon targets
- Circle the `4-6` walk-in venues for the day, do not wander
- Review the venue-specific one-line pitch and the four mandatory questions
- Dress one level sharper than the floor staff, but not formal

### Walk-in block, 1:30pm to 4:30pm
- Prioritize `Tier 1` and `Tier 2 Broadway` targets only
- Treat every walk-in as both:
  - an introduction
  - a compensation and politics fact-finding mission
- If a venue is visibly slammed, ask for the best return time and leave the resume only if they accept it

## Core walk-in script
> Hi, I’m Matt. I’m looking for a serious support role with strong earning potential, ideally barback, server assistant, or food runner. I’m local, available immediately, and comfortable with nights, weekends, and high-volume work. I wanted to introduce myself in person, ask a couple quick questions about the role, and see whether the hiring manager is available.

## Four mandatory questions
1. What does this role usually make per shift on average, not best case?
2. What do weekday shifts look like compared with Thursday through Saturday?
3. How are floors, sections, or support assignments handled for new people?
4. What is the real path and timeline to move into bartending or stronger earning shifts here?

## What to log after every serious conversation
- manager name
- exact role discussed
- stated average pay per shift
- weekday vs weekend difference
- floor / section / support assignment logic
- whether tip-out is pooled, fixed, or personality-driven
- bartender promotion timing
- management quality read
- next step and follow-up date

## Cut rules
- If they dodge average-pay questions twice, downgrade immediately
- If they say the money is only good once you get “better floors” but cannot explain how that happens, mark as political risk
- If they say everyone starts slow and there is no typical timeline to improve, stop treating it as a cash-floor play
- If the actual base-case pay sounds materially below your floor, move on
- If the manager interaction feels disorganized or evasive, do not romanticize the venue because of the name
