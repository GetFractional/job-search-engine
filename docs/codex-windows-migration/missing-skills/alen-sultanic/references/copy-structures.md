# Copy Structures

Use these as structural patterns. Adapt them. Do not paste giant swipe-file blocks unless the user explicitly asks for raw swipes.

## Binary Contrast Lead

Pattern:
- there are two types of X
- one gets the result
- one watches others get the result
- the difference is one mechanism

## Timeline Language

Pattern:
- first this
- then this
- once that happens, this becomes possible

Use when the process needs to feel ordered, guided, and inevitable.

## Assumptive Questions

Pattern starters:
- and the reason
- and that is why
- which brings us to
- the real question is
- what this really means is

Use when the copy needs to keep moving forward without sounding like a FAQ.

## External Blame, Then Responsibility

Pattern:
- it is not because you are broken, lazy, or behind
- the system, market, or old method changed
- here is the mechanism that fixes it
- here is what you do next

Use when the audience feels shame, self-blame, or overwhelm.

## Desire Stacking

Pattern:
- result one
- result two
- emotional payoff
- lifestyle payoff

Use when the offer needs to feel more vivid and desirable.

## Proof-Payoff Stack

Pattern:
- here is the future state
- here is the mechanism
- here is why it is believable
- here is the action to take now

## Diagnosis And Intrigue

Pattern:
- name the surface problem
- imply the usual advice misses the real cause
- reveal or tease the deeper mechanism

Use only when the claim can be supported.

## Value Bomb Structure

Pattern:
- teach one useful thing
- make the audience more aware
- leave them with a new distinction
- point naturally toward the next step

## Change Metaphors

Use metaphors to transfer a belief structure:
- inside-out change
- cheap vs expensive reframed through ROI
- known vs unknown
- ease vs friction

## Trial And Offer Warmth

Pattern:
- the first part is on us
- here is why verification exists
- here is what happens next
