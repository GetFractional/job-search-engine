# Core Frameworks

Use these as compact decision tools. Do not apply all of them by default.

## Simplification Hook

- Lead with how the offer removes complexity, wasted time, or needless decisions.
- Strong for founders, operators, B2B offers, and any product that risks feeling heavy.
- Ask: what becomes one page, one path, or one obvious next step?

## Change Work

- The job is not to push the sale. The job is to change belief, mood, or point of view until the decision becomes easier.
- Strong for copy, UX, sales, and content.

## Criteria Of Markets

- Read the market as a living system with current resistance, current desire, and a current buying pattern.
- Ask how people are convinced now, not how they were convinced three years ago.

## Market Quality

- A healthy market has active buyers, active sellers, repeat demand, and reachable audiences through channels you can control.
- Zero competition is usually not a green flag. It is often bad research or bad economics.

## Buyer Types

- Automatic: buys quickly.
- Repetition: needs multiple touches.
- Duration: needs time and trust over a longer window.
- Never: not worth shaping the system around.

## 3 Levels Of Customers

- Different customer levels need different product depth, price, and handholding.
- Do not force a level-3 offer onto a level-1 customer.

## Ascension Framework

- Acquire customers at break even or low friction.
- Ascend them into high-ticket or recurring value.
- Fulfill well enough that they become renewable economic assets.

## Marketing Vs Problem-Solving

- Marketing can create attention and the first sale.
- Durable businesses are built on solving real problems and producing measurable outcomes.
- If the offer is easy to market but weak at solving, retention and referrals usually suffer.

## ECA Method

- Economics: run the math first.
- Components: identify the required pieces.
- Assembly: put the pieces together fast.

## Desire Before Scarcity

- Scarcity, bonuses, and long closes are often compensation for weak desire.
- If the core offer is wanted badly enough, the close gets shorter and cleaner.

## Belief Shifting

- Horizontal: show it is possible in the world, possible for others, and possible for them.
- Vertical: show it happened before, is happening now, and will happen again, plus why.
- Useful mental model: consumption -> certainty -> conversions.

## Value Bombs And Awareness Bridge

- Value bombs are short, useful teaching moments.
- The awareness bridge is the content sequence that changes belief and prepares the buyer for the bigger ask.

## Rosetta Stone Of Offers

- Find the part of the offer or funnel already converting best.
- Deconstruct the hidden code underneath the words: constraints, desires, speed, safety, flexibility, status, ease.
- Infuse that code across the rest of the funnel.

## Ad Efficacy Matrix

- Evaluate messages on two axes:
  - realistic vs too good to be true
  - easy vs hard
- "Realistic + easy" and "realistic + hard" are usually safer than hype-heavy promises.

## Objection Marketing

- Build differential into the marketing itself.
- Clarify the hidden cost, risk, or work in competing approaches without fake attacks or fabricated claims.

## Free Trial Flip Over

- Warm framing often beats generic "free trial" language.
- Example principle: the first period is on us, then explain the verification step clearly.

## Conversion Velocity

- Lower investment and faster feedback reduce resistance.
- In product UX this means smaller asks, faster proof, faster visible wins.

## Creating Vs Running

- Creating the offer and running the offer are different phases.
- Creation work is assets, funnel, support, and system assembly.
- Running work is numbers, ads, diagnostics, follow-up, and incremental optimization.

## Component Thinking

- When performance breaks, diagnose the weak component instead of treating the whole offer as dead.
- Check expectations, order form, sales page, follow-up, call booking, fulfillment, and economics one by one.

## Compete On Economics

- Do not only ask how to spend less.
- Ask how the system can afford to pay more because the economics are stronger.

## One Thing Focus

- Great businesses and messages are often built around one clear thing done exceptionally well.
- If it cannot be explained on a napkin, it may still be too complex.

## Good Enough Is Perfect

- Perfectionism kills launches.
- Use this when the user is blocked by polishing instead of shipping.

## One Item Todo List

- When everything feels urgent, reduce the system to the single highest-leverage next move.
- Use this for execution planning, founder triage, and launch cleanup.
