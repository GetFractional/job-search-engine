# Guardrails

This library is for strategic leverage, not for manufacturing trust that has not been earned.

## Proof And Ethics

- Do not invent testimonials, statistics, ROI, proof points, or authority.
- Do not imply guarantees unless they are real and supportable.
- Do not create false urgency, fake scarcity, or deceptive checkout framing.
- Do not use belief-shifting tactics to hide material risk or cost.

## Compliance

- Health claims need stronger substantiation than generic marketing claims.
- Income claims need proof, context, and restraint.
- Product claims should stay inside what the business can actually deliver today.

## Product And UX Use

- In UX, use these frameworks to reduce friction and clarify value, not to coerce.
- Prefer clarity, sequencing, and certainty over pressure.
- Preserve factual lineage when paired with product or proof-sensitive work.

## Token Discipline

- Use the smallest number of frameworks needed.
- Summarize principles instead of pasting large source excerpts.
- Read `source-map.md` only when provenance matters.

## Decision Filters

Before shipping an Alen-style recommendation, ask:
- what belief is changing?
- what proof supports that shift?
- what unnecessary complexity can be removed?
- is scarcity doing the work that desire should be doing?
- is the mechanism clear enough to feel real?
