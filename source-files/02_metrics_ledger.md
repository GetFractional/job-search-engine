# 02-Metrics-Ledger

## Purpose

This file is the numeric source of truth for the Jobs project.

Use it to:

- validate claims before they appear in resumes, cover letters, outreach, LinkedIn copy, application answers, or interview prep
- distinguish hard facts from estimates, directional claims, and unsupported claims
- choose the safest wording for each company and metric
- prevent over-attribution or vanity framing

If a metric is not listed here, treat it as unverified until added.

---

## How to use this file

Before using any number, check:

1. **Confidence**: High, Medium, Low, or Unverified
2. **Ownership level**: direct, shared leadership, supporting, or contextual only
3. **Safe phrasing**: the preferred recruiter-facing wording
4. **Unsafe phrasing**: wording to avoid

If a role requires a metric that is not fully validated, use one of these approaches:

- remove the number and keep the business outcome
- use directional language
- explicitly frame the result as supported, contributed to, or enabled

---

## Confidence scale

### High

Matt is confident the metric is materially accurate and can explain the context.

### Medium

Metric is likely directionally accurate, but exact figure or timeframe may need reconfirmation.

### Low

Metric may be partially accurate but should not be used without careful qualification.

### Unverified

Do not use externally unless revalidated.

---

## Ownership scale

### Direct

Matt directly led or controlled the main initiative behind the result.

### Shared leadership

Matt was a major leader or cross-functional owner, but not the only driver.

### Supporting

Matt built systems or infrastructure that supported the result, but sales, operations, or other teams were primary drivers.

### Context only

Useful background, but should not be framed as Matt’s result.

---

## Wording rules

### Preferred verbs

- helped grow
- supported scale
- contributed to
- built systems that enabled
- architected infrastructure behind
- improved
- increased
- reduced
- established
- implemented

### Verbs to avoid unless clearly justified

- single-handedly drove
- owned all growth
- directly generated all revenue
- transformed the company
- built from nothing

- \$170M aggregate revenue growth
- any exact CAC claim not tied to source data
- exact LTV or churn claims
- exact paid-media ROAS claims unless documented
- any claim that implies sole ownership of sales-led company growth

## Default numeric fallback language

If certainty is incomplete, prefer:

- about
- roughly
- more than
- under
- over
- during role tenure
- supported scale from approximately
- helped build systems that enabled

When in doubt, choose the smaller, safer, more explainable claim.
