# 06-Interview-Pack-Template

## Purpose
This file is the standard structure for interview preparation inside the Jobs project.

Use it to create a role-specific interview pack after:
- the research brief is complete
- the resume is tailored
- the role is worth pursuing

This file should help convert interviews into next steps by making preparation:
- specific
- credible
- strategically useful
- easy to rehearse

---

## How to use this template
For each interview pack:
1. Copy this structure.
2. Fill it with company-specific and role-specific insight.
3. Pull proof and stories from the Canonical Profile, Metrics Ledger, and Story Bank.
4. Match answers to the primary role lane.
5. Keep the user-facing version concise enough to review before the conversation.

---

## Interview pack structure

### 1. Role snapshot
**Company:**
**Role title:**
**Primary lane:**
**Comp range:**
**Remote / logistics notes:**
**Interview stage:**
**Interviewer(s):**

### 2. Company and market summary
Cover:
- what the company does
- who it sells to
- business model
- current market context
- stage of growth or inflection
- recent signals that matter

### 3. Why this role likely exists
Write a short diagnosis of:
- what problem the company is likely trying to solve
- why they are hiring now
- what friction or constraint probably exists
- what success likely looks like in 6 to 12 months

### 4. Matt’s strongest fit angle
Summarize in 3 to 5 bullets:
- why Matt fits this role
- why his background is unusually relevant
- which company experiences are most useful here
- what likely differentiates him from other candidates
- why the mandate is a fit even if the title is broader than his most obvious lane

### 5. Likely interviewer concerns
List likely objections or concerns such as:
- title mismatch
- broad marketing title vs actual operator fit
- breadth vs specialization
- sales-led context attribution
- formal degree filters
- short tenure concerns
- industry background mismatch

Then prepare a calm response strategy for each.

### 6. Opening answer: “Tell me about yourself”
Build a 60-second and 2-minute version.

Structure:
- who Matt is professionally
- the kind of work he does best
- 2 to 3 relevant proof points
- why this role fits now

### 7. Core interview questions
Build likely questions and draft strong answers.

#### Recommended question categories
- background and fit
- systems and execution
- leadership and collaboration
- growth and measurement
- lifecycle / CRM / RevOps depth
- ecommerce, CRO, and owned-channel growth depth
- broader marketing-leadership and mandate-fit depth
- AI and automation depth
- conflict / influence / judgment
- why this company / role
- why now / why leaving

## Standard question bank
Use only the most relevant questions for each role.

### Background and fit
1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Why are you interested in this role?
3. Why this company?
4. Why now?
5. Why are you looking to leave your current situation?

### Systems and execution
6. Tell me about a time you built or redesigned a system that improved execution.
7. How do you diagnose where growth is actually breaking down?
8. How do you decide what to automate versus what to keep manual?
9. How do you improve cross-functional handoffs?
10. What does strong operating cadence look like to you?

### RevOps / GTM / lifecycle
11. How have you improved CRM, lifecycle, or reporting infrastructure?
12. What have you done to improve visibility across the funnel?
13. How do you think about lifecycle marketing beyond campaigns?
14. How do you align marketing, sales, and operations?
15. How do you define and measure success in a growth system?

### Leadership and collaboration
16. How do you lead without authority?
17. Tell me about a time you had to influence leadership.
18. How do you manage vendors or cross-functional teams?
19. What kind of team environments bring out your best work?
20. Tell me about a disagreement with a stakeholder and how you handled it.

### Ecommerce / CRO / owned-channel growth
21. Tell me about an ecommerce business you helped stabilize or grow.
22. How do you think about CRO beyond just running tests?
23. How do you decide where to focus first across traffic, conversion, AOV, and retention?
24. What have you done to improve onsite merchandising visibility and promotional execution?
25. How do you evaluate whether a promotional calendar is actually working?
26. How do you think about SEO, lifecycle, and onsite UX working together in ecommerce?
27. Tell me about a time you improved ecommerce performance without relying primarily on paid media.
28. How do you use analytics across Shopify, GA4, heatmaps, and onsite tools to diagnose performance?

### Broader marketing-leadership / mandate fit
29. You have held titles like Director of Marketing. What kind of marketing leadership roles are actually the best fit for you?
30. How do you handle broad marketing roles that expect both strategy and execution?
31. Tell me about a time you led a marketing function and improved performance through systems, not just activity.
32. How do you evaluate whether a Director or Head of Marketing role is really a growth-systems role or mostly a brand role?
33. Tell me about a time you had to adapt positioning or channel mix because market conditions changed.

### AI and automation
34. How are you actually using AI in your work?
35. What is the difference between AI hype and useful AI implementation?
36. Tell me about a workflow you improved with AI or automation.
37. How do you think about AI governance or quality control?

### Judgment and resilience
38. Tell me about a failure or pivot and what you learned.
39. What is a growth metric you would not trust at face value?
40. How do you make decisions when information is incomplete?
41. Tell me about a time you reduced complexity.

## Answer-building rules
For each answer:
- be specific
- use one strong example
- explain the business problem first
- then describe what Matt built or changed
- then explain the result
- then close with why that matters here

Preferred answer flow:
**Context → diagnosis → action → system built → result → lesson / relevance**

Avoid:
- long autobiographies
- jargon dumping
- channel laundry lists
- vague strategic statements with no operating detail
- over-claiming sole ownership when results were shared

## Broader marketing-title objection prompts
Use these when the title is Director of Marketing, Head of Marketing, Senior Director of Marketing, or a similarly broad leadership title.

### Common marketing-title objections
- Your background looks more growth / ops heavy than traditional marketing.
- This title is broad. Are you really comfortable owning a wider marketing function?
- You seem strong in systems and performance, but how do you handle broader team leadership and execution?
- Are you a fit for this role if the title says Marketing rather than Growth, Revenue, or Operations?

### Best response themes
- Lead with Bob’s Watches because it is direct evidence of succeeding in a Director of Marketing role.
- Use Affordable Insurance Quotes when the role values broad ownership across marketing, systems, GTM, and operations.
- Use Breakthrough Academy when executive influence, channel shift, team coordination, and positioning judgment matter.
- Be explicit that the right fit is determined by mandate, not by title alone.
- Make clear that your strongest marketing roles are performance-heavy, systems-oriented, revenue-accountable, and execution-driven.

## Ecommerce-specific objection prompts
Use these when the role sits in Lane 5 or has strong ecommerce components.

### Common ecommerce objections
- You seem more systems / ops-heavy than pure ecommerce.
- Most of your experience sounds broader than ecommerce merchandising.
- How deep are you really in Shopify / CRO / lifecycle execution?
- You have strong SEO and systems experience, but how hands-on are you with promotional cadence and onsite conversion?
- Have you owned ecommerce growth in a normalized market, not just a boom period?

### Best response themes
- Lead with OC Ramps as a post-COVID normalization and stabilization story.
- Use Bob’s Watches for larger-scale ecommerce revenue and CRM proof.
- Be clear that your ecommerce strength is growth systems, CRO, lifecycle, analytics, and owned-channel execution, not inventory planning or Amazon-heavy merchandising.
- Use numbers from the Metrics Ledger, not improvised estimates.

## Questions Matt should ask
Build 6 to 10 smart questions total. Prioritize quality over volume.

### Role and mandate
- What problem is this role meant to solve in the next 6 to 12 months?
- What would make someone clearly successful in this role by the end of year one?
- What is working today, and what is not working well enough yet?

### Systems and execution
- Where does execution currently break down most often across the funnel or customer journey?
- How mature are the team’s reporting, handoff, and operating systems today?
- What systems or processes are strong already, and what still feels fragmented?

### Team and collaboration
- Which functions would this role need to align most closely with?
- Where do cross-functional handoffs tend to slow things down today?

### Strategy and context
- Why is the role open now?
- What has changed in the business that makes this hire especially important?

### Director / Head / VP of Marketing questions, when relevant
- How much of this role is tied to measurable performance versus brand stewardship?
- Where does the team need stronger operating rhythm, visibility, or accountability today?
- Is this role expected to act more like a growth / performance operator, a functional marketing leader, or both?
- Which areas are strongest today, and which parts of the function still feel fragmented?
- How much of the mandate is strategy, and how much is improving day-to-day execution quality?

### Ecommerce-specific questions, when relevant
- Where do you see the biggest gap today across traffic quality, conversion rate, AOV, and retention?
- How do you currently evaluate promotional performance beyond top-line revenue?
- How mature is the lifecycle / CRM program today?
- What parts of the onsite experience feel strongest, and where do you see the most friction?
- How do you currently use Shopify, GA4, heatmaps, testing tools, and BI reporting in decision-making?
- How dependent is growth today on paid channels versus owned and organic channels?
- Are merchandising, CRO, lifecycle, and content operating as one system or mostly in silos?

### AI and leverage, if relevant
- Where do you see the best opportunity to use automation or AI to improve execution quality or speed?

## Closing statement prep
Prepare a short closing statement for the end of the interview.

Template:
Thanks, this was helpful. The more I understand the role, the more it seems aligned with the kind of work I do best, especially around [specific mandate]. I’d be excited to help the team build a stronger [systems / lifecycle / RevOps / growth] foundation if there’s a fit.

Director / Head / VP of Marketing variation:
Thanks, this was helpful. The more I understand the role, the more it seems like the title is broad but the mandate is really about improving performance, systems, and execution quality. That is the kind of marketing leadership work I tend to do best, and I’d be excited to help the team build a stronger foundation if there’s a fit.

Ecommerce variation:
Thanks, this was helpful. The more I understand the role, the more it seems aligned with the kind of ecommerce work I do best, especially where CRO, lifecycle, onsite experience, and reporting need to work together more deliberately. I’d be excited to help the team build a stronger ecommerce growth system if there’s a fit.

## Final review checklist
Before using an interview pack, confirm:
- the role lane is correct
- the top 3 stories are the right ones
- numbers match the Metrics Ledger
- the “tell me about yourself” answer is tailored
- likely objections are addressed
- questions to ask are company-specific
- for ecommerce roles, OC Ramps or Bob’s Watches is used intentionally rather than generically
- for Director / Head / VP of Marketing roles, Bob’s Watches, Affordable Insurance Quotes, Breakthrough Academy, or OC Ramps is used based on mandate rather than title alone
- tone is calm, sharp, and credible

When in doubt:
- shorten the answer
- add more diagnosis
- remove one claim
- make the example clearer
