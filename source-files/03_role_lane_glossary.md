# 03-Role-Lane-Glossary

## Purpose

This file routes job titles into the correct role lane so the Jobs project can:

- choose the right resume architecture
- choose the right proof points
- avoid broad, generic positioning
- improve fit-based application quality

Use this file before building any role-specific asset.

---

## How to use this file

For each new role:

1. Match the exact title to the closest lane.
2. Read the title-family notes.
3. Check whether the role should be pursued aggressively, pursued, handled selectively, or passed.
4. Use the proof themes and objections listed for that lane.
5. If the title is ambiguous, classify by mandate, not title alone.

---

## Lane 1: Revenue / Growth Operations

### Positioning

Best lane. Use when the company needs systems, visibility, handoffs, automation, reporting, lifecycle infrastructure, or cross-functional GTM execution.

### Core titles

- Director of Revenue Operations
- Head of Revenue Operations
- Director of Growth Operations
- Head of Growth Operations
- Director of GTM Operations
- Revenue Operations Lead
- GTM Operations Lead
- Director of Business Operations, Growth
- Director of Commercial Operations
- Director of Marketing Operations, if it clearly extends into GTM and revenue infrastructure

### Adjacent titles that may map here

- Director of Sales Operations, if role spans lifecycle, reporting, automation, and GTM handoffs
- Director of Business Systems, if role is growth / revenue adjacent
- Revenue Enablement Lead, if role includes systems and lifecycle, not only training

### Why this lane fits Matt

- strongest match to CRM architecture, lifecycle systems, reporting, enablement, automation, process design, and operational visibility
- strongest fit to companies with traction but underdeveloped infrastructure
- most defensible lane for cross-functional operator identity

### Proof themes to prioritize

- HireHawk AI-assisted recruiting / RevOps buildout
- Prosper lifecycle, partner ops, enablement, and reporting systems
- Affordable Insurance Quotes AMS and Zoho operating stack
- SkyFineUSA ERP and process architecture

### Typical hiring pains

- inconsistent funnel definitions
- weak handoffs between teams
- poor reporting and visibility
- manual workflows
- unclear operating cadence
- fragmented GTM systems
- scaling complexity without systems discipline

### Default pursue level

- Pursue aggressively if comp and logistics fit

### Main risks / objections

- recruiter may see “marketing” before “operations” if resume is not retitled correctly
- degree-sensitive ops roles may still filter hard
- if title is too sales-ops-only and not broader GTM / lifecycle / CRM, fit may weaken

### Resume emphasis

- systems built
- automation
- reporting and dashboards
- enablement and onboarding
- CRM / lifecycle / handoff design
- execution quality and visibility

## Lane 4: Director / Head / VP of Marketing

### Positioning

Selective lane, but broader than the title suggests. Use when the role is clearly performance-heavy, revenue or pipeline accountable, systems-oriented, and lifecycle / RevOps / ecommerce-growth adjacent. Do not downgrade fit just because the title says Marketing rather than Growth or Revenue.

### Core titles

- Director of Marketing
- Senior Director of Marketing
- Head of Marketing
- VP of Marketing
- Vice President, Marketing
- Director of Digital Marketing, when full-funnel and systems-heavy
- Director of Performance Marketing, when broader than channel management
- Head of Growth
- VP of Growth
- Senior Director / VP, Revenue Marketing

### Why this lane may fit

- very relevant for smaller and mid-sized companies that use broad marketing titles for roles that are really growth-system leadership roles
- good when the company wants someone to wire the house, not just own narrative
- strong fit when the mandate includes performance, lifecycle, CRM, reporting, ecommerce growth, team leadership, and execution systems under one owner
- stronger when the company needs someone who can influence positioning, channel mix, and execution systems during changing market conditions

### Why this lane is riskier

- broad title invites broader expectations
- some roles are really brand / comms / product marketing leadership under a growth-sounding title
- bigger companies may filter more heavily on pedigree, degree, or narrower prior title matching
- some Director of Marketing roles are actually channel-manager roles, while others are near-CMO operator roles, so mandate matters more than title

### Pursue if role has most of these traits

- revenue, pipeline, or ecommerce-performance accountability
- lifecycle / CRM / demand / analytics / reporting depth in the JD
- systems, automation, CRO, or GTM alignment language
- player-coach expectations
- post-traction business with real operating complexity
- need for stronger operating rhythm across channels, teams, or funnel stages

### Default pursue level

- Pursue when Director / Head of Marketing is clearly a growth-systems, performance, or ecommerce-operator role
- Selective / opportunistic when VP of Marketing is broader and more executive than operator
- Pass only when the role is clearly brand-led, PR-led, product-marketing-led, or too disconnected from measurable execution

### Main risks / objections

- “too broad” perception
- title inflation concerns if resume is not tightly scoped
- recruiter assumptions that Matt is more operator than enterprise marketing executive
- title confusion if the resume does not make the business mandate obvious quickly

### Resume emphasis

- growth systems leadership
- operating cadence
- revenue or ecommerce-performance accountability
- team leadership with execution depth
- executive influence and positioning judgment
- CRM, lifecycle, CRO, and reporting integration
- AI + systems leverage where relevant

- Matt understands ecommerce growth systems, traffic mix, promotional calendars, onsite merchandising visibility, email / lifecycle, CRO, analytics, and web / vendor coordination
- strongest fit is for ecommerce roles centered on owned-channel growth and conversion systems, not inventory-heavy merchandising leadership

### Proof themes to prioritize

- Bob’s Watches revenue growth from \$20.5M to \$45M, CRM / lifecycle systems, BI dashboards, list growth, and omnichannel execution
- OC Ramps fractional ecommerce leadership across campaign calendar, CRO, SEO, GA4 / GTM, BI reporting, site rebuild, financing visibility, zero-party data capture, and content / custom-build funnel strategy

## Title-routing rules

### Route by mandate, not title alone

If the title is ambiguous, classify based on what the company actually needs.

### Strong mandate signals for Lane 1

- RevOps
- GTM ops
- lifecycle + CRM + reporting
- enablement + onboarding + systems
- automation + dashboards + handoffs

### Strong mandate signals for Lane 2

- retention
- engagement
- CRM
- member journey
- onboarding
- segmentation
- lifecycle

### Strong mandate signals for Lane 3

- pipeline
- SQLs
- demand generation
- CRO
- experimentation
- revenue marketing
- funnel optimization
- acquisition + lifecycle integration
- paid + owned channel coordination
- positioning or messaging adaptation tied to performance

### Strong mandate signals for Lane 4

- Director / Head / VP of Marketing title paired with performance, pipeline, ecommerce, lifecycle, CRM, or reporting ownership
- broad cross-channel team leadership with measurable execution expectations
- player-coach scope
- post-traction company needing one operator to connect strategy, execution, and systems
- marketing leadership role that is really a growth-systems role under a broader title

### Strong mandate signals for Lane 5

- Shopify or ecommerce stack ownership
- CRO
- retention / lifecycle
- onsite merchandising visibility
- promotional calendar ownership
- product page / cart / checkout optimization
- SEO + content tied to commerce
- owned-channel growth
- paid + owned coordination
- GA4 / GTM / ecommerce analytics
- quiz / personalization / zero-party data capture
- site rebuild or ecommerce UX improvement

### Director / Head of Marketing routing rule

Do not pass Director of Marketing or Head of Marketing by title alone.

When those titles show up:
- route to Lane 3 if the role is growth / demand / funnel heavy
- route to Lane 4 if the role is broader marketing leadership with strong performance and systems ownership
- route to Lane 5 if the role is ecommerce, lifecycle, CRO, onsite experience, or owned-channel heavy
- pass only if the mandate is mostly brand, PR, communications, or product marketing

### Mandate signals that usually weaken fit

- pure brand awareness
- PR / communications
- analyst relations
- product marketing only
- corporate messaging without execution systems ownership

## Fast pass / no-pass rules

### Usually pass if

- role is clearly pure brand
- role is clearly PR/comms
- role is clearly product marketing only
- company is extremely early and chaotic with no traction
- compensation is far below floor and there is no compelling upside
- role requires deep on-site presence that is not workable

### Usually pursue if

- role needs systems, automation, reporting, lifecycle, handoffs, or measurable execution
- company has traction but weak operating infrastructure
- JD values cross-functional execution and business outcomes
- AI and automation are seen as leverage, not novelty

## Lane priority order

Default priority order for active search:

1. Revenue / Growth Operations
2. Ecommerce / DTC Growth
3. Growth / Revenue Marketing
4. Lifecycle / CRM / Retention
5. Director / Head / VP of Marketing, selective but viable

Why this order works:

- Revenue / Growth Operations is still the sharpest and most defensible lane
- Ecommerce / DTC Growth is now a strong secondary lane because Bob’s Watches plus OC Ramps creates real, current proof
- Growth / Revenue Marketing remains strong because your background spans funnel systems, demand, CRO, reporting, paid + owned coordination, and positioning under market change
- Lifecycle / CRM / Retention is still strong, but often better used as a sub-angle inside broader growth or ecommerce roles
- Director / Head of Marketing should not be underweighted; many of those titles are actually strong-fit operator roles when the mandate is performance-heavy and systems-oriented

If conversion data later shows a different winning lane, update this order.
