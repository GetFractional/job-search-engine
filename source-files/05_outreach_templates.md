# 05-Outreach-Templates

## Purpose

This file provides reusable outreach structures for the Jobs project.

Use these templates to:

- increase response rates
- show fit quickly
- sound human, sharp, and relevant
- avoid generic, spammy, or over-eager outreach

Do not send these word-for-word by default.
Always tailor to the company, role, and person.

---

## Outreach principles

### The goal

The goal is not to tell my whole story.
The goal is to create enough curiosity and credibility to earn the next step.

### The style

Use outreach that is:

- short
- specific
- relevant
- calm
- credible
- easy to answer

### Do not do this

- over-explain
- dump full resume content into a message
- sound desperate
- sound like a chatbot
- sound overly polished or performative
- pretend familiarity that does not exist
- ask for too much in the first message

### What strong outreach does

- shows why I am reaching out
- shows why the role or company is relevant
- gives 1 to 3 specific reasons I fit
- makes the next step easy

## Core messaging anchor
Use this positioning as the default message spine unless a role requires a narrower angle.

I build the systems behind measurable growth.

My strongest work sits at the intersection of revenue operations, growth operations, lifecycle, CRM, automation, reporting, CRO, and ecommerce growth. I do my best work when a company has real traction but needs better infrastructure behind execution, clearer visibility across the funnel, and a more scalable operating rhythm.

Across ecommerce, telecom, insurance, education, agencies, and startup environments, I’ve helped build systems that supported massive scale.

Default proof stack:
- enabled scale from roughly $45M to $120M+ at Prosper Wireless in a sales-led telecom business
- built the strategies, systems, and team at Bob’s Watches to help scale from $20.5M to $45M in 24 months while maintaining roughly 31% margin
- built a custom AMS and GTM affiliate strategy at Affordable Insurance Quotes to help scale from about $290K to $2M in 18 months while generating 30K+ leads at a 55% conversion rate and helping secure a $5M LOI

AI is a meaningful differentiator, not the headline. Use it to reinforce leverage, execution speed, workflow design, and decision quality.

## Message architecture by use case

### Connect note

Goal: earn the connection.

### Follow-up after connect

Goal: give light context and suggest fit.

### Recruiter email

Goal: show relevance, clarity, and ease.

### Hiring manager

- show diagnosis, not just fit
- use fewer but stronger proof points
- avoid over-explaining your entire background
- include a sharper company-specific reason for interest when possible

### Founder / executive version

Hi [Name], I came across [Company] while reviewing the [Role Title] opportunity. The role appears tied to the kind of [growth systems / lifecycle / GTM ops] work I do best, so I wanted to connect.

### Rules

- Keep under the platform limit.
- Do not cram proof points into the connect note.
- Aim for relevance, not persuasion.

## Template 8: Why this company / why now line bank

Use one sentence only. Tailor to the company’s likely stage, constraint, and mandate.

### General operator versions

- The role stands out because it appears tied to operational scale, not just top-of-funnel activity.
- What makes the opportunity interesting is the apparent need to connect growth, lifecycle, and reporting into a more scalable system.
- From the outside, the company looks like it has real traction and now needs stronger infrastructure behind execution.
- The mandate seems less about adding more activity and more about building better systems behind results.
- What caught my attention is that the role appears to sit at the point where growth becomes a systems problem, not just a channel problem.

### Revenue / Growth Ops versions

- The role seems especially relevant because it appears tied to improving visibility, handoffs, and execution across the revenue engine.
- What stands out is the likely need for someone who can connect CRM, lifecycle, reporting, and operating cadence into a cleaner system.
- From the outside, it looks like the business has enough motion that better infrastructure could unlock disproportionate gains.
- The opportunity seems well matched to the kind of work I do best: reducing operational friction behind growth and revenue execution.

### Lifecycle / CRM versions

- What makes the opportunity interesting is the likely need to improve lifecycle performance through better segmentation, messaging, and customer-journey systems.
- The role appears tied to building a more deliberate retention and engagement engine rather than simply sending more campaigns.
- From the outside, it seems like the company has enough scale that better lifecycle orchestration could materially improve activation, retention, or LTV.

### Growth / Revenue Marketing versions

- The role stands out because it appears focused on measurable growth performance, not just marketing activity.
- What caught my attention is the need to connect acquisition, lifecycle, and reporting into a more accountable growth engine.
- The opportunity looks especially relevant because it seems tied to improving funnel performance, conversion, and revenue visibility at the same time.

### Director / Head / VP of Marketing versions

- The title is broad, but the role appears tied to performance, accountability, and stronger execution systems rather than brand activity alone.
- What makes the opportunity interesting is that it seems to require someone who can connect strategy, execution, reporting, and team operating rhythm under one owner.
- From the outside, the role looks less like a traditional marketing title and more like a growth-systems leadership position under a broader label.
- The opportunity stood out because it appears to need a player-coach operator who can improve execution quality, visibility, and performance across the function.

### Ecommerce / DTC Growth versions

- What makes the opportunity interesting is the apparent need to improve ecommerce performance through a stronger mix of CRO, lifecycle, SEO, and site experience.
- The role seems well matched to the kind of ecommerce work I’ve done best: building more deliberate growth systems across campaign planning, onsite conversion, owned channels, and reporting.
- From the outside, it looks like the business may benefit from a more integrated approach to ecommerce growth, where traffic, conversion, lifecycle, and onsite merchandising work together instead of in silos.
- The opportunity stood out because it appears tied to building a stronger ecommerce operating system, not just driving more promotional activity.

### Good reasons to reference when true

Use only if supported by research:

- recent product or category expansion
- signs of post-traction scaling complexity
- a visible site / funnel experience gap
- evidence of heavy paid dependence with weak owned-channel leverage
- a recent leadership hire, fundraise, or strategic shift
- an underdeveloped lifecycle or CRM motion
- an ecommerce brand that appears to have demand but weak conversion infrastructure

## Outreach rules by recipient

### Recruiter

- be clear and easy to scan
- use title fit, lane fit, and proof fit
- keep to 100 to 170 words when possible

### Hiring manager

- show diagnosis, not just fit
- use fewer but stronger proof points
- avoid over-explaining your entire background

### Founder / executive

- be concise and thoughtful
- frame around business problem and leverage
- avoid buzzwords

### Peer / connector

- make the ask low-friction
- provide a forwardable blurb when useful

---

## Tone rules

Use:

- grounded
- direct
- calm
- human
- specific
- helpful

Avoid:

- hype
- desperation
- flattery
- jargon stuffing
- fake familiarity
- corny open loops
- overuse of “chaos” language

Preferred phrases:

- systems behind measurable growth
- stronger infrastructure behind execution
- clearer visibility across the funnel
- more scalable operating rhythm
- operational friction
- fragmented systems
- stronger visibility
- scalable execution
- measurable growth infrastructure
- lifecycle and GTM systems

---

## Fast assembly guidance

When building outreach, combine these pieces in order:

1. recipient-specific opener
2. one strong why-this-company or why-this-role line
3. 1 to 3 lane-matched proof points
4. easy next step

For ecommerce roles, strongly consider using:

- one OC Ramps proof point
- one Bob’s Watches proof point
- one systems or lifecycle proof point

For Director / Head / VP of Marketing roles, do not underweight the role because of title alone. If the mandate is performance-heavy, systems-oriented, revenue-accountable, or ecommerce-driven, use the stronger marketing-leadership proof stack.

For RevOps / lifecycle roles, use OC Ramps only when the company values owned-channel systems, CRO, or site-experience leadership.

## Final guidance

Good outreach earns a conversation because it feels relevant, not because it feels clever.

When in doubt:

- shorten it
- sharpen it
- give value
- remove one claim
- make the next step easier
