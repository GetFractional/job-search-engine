# 07-LinkedIn-Optimization-Brief

## Purpose

This file keeps Matt’s LinkedIn aligned with the Jobs project.

Use it to:

- align LinkedIn with the lead role lane
- improve recruiter search relevance
- make the profile feel sharper, more coherent, and more credible
- support outreach and applications with consistent positioning

The default goal is not to be clever.
The goal is to be clear, relevant, and search-friendly.

---

## Primary recommendation

Default LinkedIn positioning should align to **Revenue / Growth Operations** while clearly supporting **Ecommerce / DTC Growth** as a credible secondary lane and **AI-enabled systems leadership** as a meaningful differentiator.

This means the profile should center on:

- Revenue Operations
- Growth Operations
- GTM Operations
- Lifecycle / CRM
- Automation
- Reporting / visibility
- measurable execution

It should also support ecommerce search relevance through selected language around:

- Ecommerce Growth
- CRO
- SEO
- owned-channel growth
- Shopify / GA4 / reporting
- site experience
- lifecycle and retention

It should also support AI relevance through selected language around:

- AI Automation
- AI-assisted workflows
- Agentic AI
- workflow automation
- systems design
- operator-level AI implementation
- AI-enabled research and execution

Do not center the profile on generic “marketing leader” language unless targeting a specific selective Head / VP of Marketing role.

Do not center the profile on ecommerce merchandising, inventory planning, or Amazon-heavy leadership unless a specific role requires it.

## LinkedIn objectives

The profile should do four things well:

1. make recruiter search matching easier
2. reduce confusion about what Matt is best suited for
3. make the profile feel more credible than flashy
4. support conversation conversion after outreach or applications

## About section strategy

### Goal

The About section should quickly answer:

- who Matt is professionally
- what problem he solves
- what kinds of systems he builds
- what proof supports that
- what roles he is targeting

### Best structure

#### Paragraph 1

State the professional identity and problem solved.

#### Paragraph 2

Summarize what kinds of systems Matt builds.

#### Paragraph 3

Give 3 to 5 high-signal proof points.

#### Paragraph 4

Mention AI / automation depth in concrete operator language.

#### Paragraph 5

State the types of roles being targeted.

### Draft About section

This should stay closely aligned to the current live summary unless a role-specific version is needed.

I build the systems behind measurable growth.

My strongest work sits at the intersection of revenue operations, growth operations, lifecycle, CRM, automation, reporting, CRO, and e-commerce growth.

I do my best work when a company has real traction but needs better infrastructure behind execution, clearer visibility across the funnel, and a more scalable operating rhythm. Across ecommerce, telecom, insurance, education, agencies, and startup environments, I’ve helped build systems that supported massive scale.

Some highlights:
- Enabled scale from $45M to $120M+ in Prosper Wireless, a sales-led telecom business.
- Built the strategies, systems, and team at Bob’s Watches to scale from $20.5M to $45M in 24 months while maintaining a 31% margin.
- Built a custom AMS and GTM affiliate strategy to scale Affordable Insurance Quotes from about $290K to $2M in 18 months while generating 30K+ leads at a 55% conversion rate. I helped secure a $5M LOI from a nationwide P&C firm.

I also leverage AI to maximize efficiency and efficacy. I’ve spent the last three years using AI daily across both professional and personal workflows, including generative and agentic systems. That experience includes AI-assisted workflow design, research, evaluation, automation, and product development using tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Codex.

Right now, I’m most interested in leadership roles across Revenue Operations, Growth Operations, GTM Operations, Ecommerce Growth, Lifecycle / CRM, and systems-oriented Growth / Revenue Marketing.

## LinkedIn content guidance

If posting publicly while job searching:

- favor thoughtful operator insight over self-promotion
- share lessons on systems, lifecycle, AI workflows, execution, hiring, ecommerce growth, CRO, and clarity
- avoid sounding like you are publicly begging for a role
- avoid posts that could weaken your positioning or read as reactive

Good ecommerce-related post themes:

- what most brands get wrong about promotional calendars
- how CRO is more than testing button colors
- why owned channels matter more when paid gets expensive
- what post-COVID normalization teaches about ecommerce discipline
- what stronger reporting changes in ecommerce decision-making

Good AI-related post themes:

- the difference between AI hype and useful workflow automation
- where agentic AI actually creates leverage in operating roles
- how to use AI to improve decision quality, not just content speed
- what most teams misunderstand about AI implementation
- how AI fits into RevOps, lifecycle, recruiting, and ecommerce execution
