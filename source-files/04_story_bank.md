# 04-Story-Bank

## Purpose
This file stores reusable stories for:
- interviews
- application answers
- cover letters
- recruiter conversations
- networking outreach
- LinkedIn content, when relevant

Use this file to select the right story for the right lane.
Do not default to the most impressive story. Default to the most relevant story.

---

## How to use this file
For each role:
1. Identify the primary lane.
2. Identify the company’s likely pain.
3. Select 2 to 4 stories that best match the mandate.
4. Tailor the framing to the job.
5. Prefer clear problem → system built → result → lesson.

---

## Story structure guide
Each story should answer:
- What was broken, unclear, or constrained?
- What did Matt diagnose?
- What system, process, or infrastructure did he build?
- What changed as a result?
- Why does that matter for this role?

Preferred answer style:
- concise version for screens and applications
- expanded version for interviews

## Story selection rules
- Pick relevance before prestige.
- Prefer 2 strong stories over 6 scattered stories.
- For screens, keep stories concise and outcome-led.
- For interviews, expand into diagnosis, system built, result, and lesson.
- When attribution is shared, say so.
- If a role is highly operational, do not lead with broad marketing language.
- If a role is lifecycle-heavy, lead with communications scale, segmentation, and customer journey systems.
- Do not underuse a story just because the title says Marketing. If the mandate is performance-heavy, systems-oriented, ecommerce-driven, or revenue-accountable, Bob’s Watches, Affordable Insurance Quotes, Breakthrough Academy, and OC Ramps may all be strong choices.
